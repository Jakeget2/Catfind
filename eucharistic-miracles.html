Famous Eucharistic Miracles from Around the World

  • Lanciano, Italy (8th Century)

    This is one of the earliest and most famous Eucharistic miracles. During Mass, a priest doubted the Real Presence, and the consecrated Host transformed into actual flesh and blood, which has been preserved and can still be seen today.

  • Buenos Aires, Argentina (1996)

    A consecrated Host was found to have transformed into a piece of human heart tissue. Scientific analysis revealed it to be living heart muscle with blood-type AB.

  • Sokółka, Poland (2008)

    A piece of the Eucharist that fell to the ground was found to have transformed into human tissue during a Mass. Investigations confirmed it was indeed cardiac tissue.

  • Treviso, Italy (1171)

    During a Mass, a priest accidentally dropped the consecrated Host, which subsequently began to bleed. The miraculous Host has been preserved in a glass case since then.

  • Edmonton, Canada (1991)

    During a Eucharistic procession, a Host was found on the ground and discovered to be pulsating and bleeding. It was later analyzed and confirmed to be human cardiac tissue.
